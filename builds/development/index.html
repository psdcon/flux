how does flux do this?

Flux is built on a single database that enables users to have a personalised dashboard showing all relevant activities occurring in real time on any device, anywhere in the world.

core components of the system reflect the requirements of any business:

Accounts, including real time trial balance, balance sheet, P&L, and cash-flow
Stock management
CRM
Invoicing/contracts
Analytics
Integrated electronic payment system
Web based HTML5 Customer/Supplier interface
Workflow management
Employee analytics


Once a new sale is made, stocks, invoicing, accounts, and workflow all update in real time without any user involvement. The single database means that this happens without any lag regardless of the device or location that the sale is recorded on. Having a unified database also results in data only needing to be inputted once - a major time saver compared with today’s traditional ERP systems.

All transactions are constantly monitored and compared by Flux. If a suspicious looking transaction occurs, the relevant user is notified immediately to assess the issue.

comex mckinnon case study

Prior to the implementation of Flux, Comex McKinnon was a mid-tier Irish agricultural supply chain management company with turnover of € 108m, handling 450,000 tonnes of product annually. Following the implementation of Flux in 2011, the company began to grow rapidly. By 2013, the company had turnover of € 203m, handling 1,000,000 tonnes of product with one less employee. Due to Flux’s transparent nature, Comex was also trading with less risk and increased profitability.

key facts

€203m turnover with 13 employees
€400m of transactions overseen by one accounts staff
1,000,000 tonnes of stock physically moved globally using shipping and trucking with one stock controller and one logistics manager
88% revenue growth in three years since the implementation of Flux
